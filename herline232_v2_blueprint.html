The Fundamental Shift

From reporting pipe to active ecosystem

Version 1 of HERLINE 232 was a strong reporting and routing system. Good - but not enough to win. The critical upgrade is that HERLINE 232 now operates across three distinct layers: we receive, we respond directly, and only then do we escalate to heavier implementing bodies what we cannot handle ourselves. Experts at the Canadian High Commission will immediately ask what we DO with what we receive. This blueprint answers that question completely.

Layer 1 - Ours

Receive

HERLINE 232 owns this entirely. All five channels - SMS, USSD, WhatsApp, Voice IVR, Web - feed into our intake system. Anonymous, available 24/7, no smartphone needed. Every report is triaged, assigned a case token, and logged into our anonymized database within 60 seconds of contact.

SMS short codeUSSD *232#WhatsApp botVoice IVRWeb portal
Layer 2 - Ours

Respond

This is what version 1 was missing. HERLINE 232 does not simply pass cases on - we actively respond first. This includes: automated triage and safety assessment, anonymous expert consultation sessions (scheduled directly through the platform), follow-up SMS check-ins at 7 and 30 days, legal information delivery, community health worker outreach for unresponsive high-risk cases, and data-driven advocacy report generation. We hold the case until it is genuinely resolved or appropriately escalated.

Expert consultationsCHW outreachFollow-up protocolLegal info deliveryAdvocacy reporting
Layer 3 - Escalate

Refer

Only when a case requires physical intervention, medical treatment, legal prosecution, or long-term shelter that HERLINE 232 cannot provide, do we escalate - with full case documentation - to the appropriate implementing body. Rainbo Initiative (medical/forensic), AdvocAid (legal), Police FRU (emergency), One-Stop Centres (psychosocial), or MoGCA (policy). We do not disappear after referral - we retain follow-up responsibility and track outcomes.

Signature Feature - Expert Consultation

The SafeVoice Expert Network

Anonymous · Scheduled · Verified · Free

Talk to an expert.
Without revealing who you are.

Survivors and community members can schedule a confidential one-on-one session with a verified expert - a counsellor, legal advisor, medical professional, or social worker - directly through the HERLINE 232 platform. Only their location district is known. No name. No phone number stored. No record that could endanger them. The session is real, human, and consequential.

How scheduling works

Via SMS, USSD menu, or WhatsApp. Survivor selects expert type, preferred district, and time slot. Receives a session code. Expert calls the platform - not the survivor directly. Platform bridges anonymously.

What the expert knows

Only: district, issue category (legal / medical / psychosocial / safety), and language preference. No name. No phone number. No identifying details unless survivor chooses to share during session.

Session outcome

Expert logs anonymized session notes. Recommends next steps. If escalation needed - with survivor consent - case is transferred to implementing body with documented context. No cold handoffs.

Expert types available through the SafeVoice network:

Psychosocial counsellors

Trauma-informed counselling for rape, domestic violence, and FGM/C survivors. Sessions in Krio, English, Temne, Mende.

Legal paralegals

Explain rights under the Sexual Offences Act 2012, child marriage law, and how to pursue prosecution safely. From AdvocAid network.

Medical advisors

Advise on emergency contraception timelines, STI prevention, evidence preservation, and nearest treatment facility access.

Community safety advisors

For callers reporting a girl at risk of forced marriage or FGM ceremony. Advise on intervention, safe reporting, and community protocol.

Child protection officers

For cases involving minors - under-18 survivors, children in dangerous home environments, child marriage situations.

Economic empowerment advisors

For survivors needing to understand livelihood options, safe housing, or how to leave an abusive domestic situation financially.

How a session happens - step by step:

1

Survivor requests a session

Via SMS ("TALK" to short code), USSD menu option 3, or WhatsApp. Selects expert type and available time slot shown in their district. Receives a 6-digit anonymous session code.

2

Platform notifies the expert

Expert receives an alert via the NGO web portal: district, issue type, language, scheduled time. Expert does not see any personal information. Expert confirms availability or system re-assigns.

3

At session time - anonymous bridge call

Survivor dials a session number using their session code. Expert dials in separately. Platform bridges the two anonymously using voice masking. Neither party's real number is exposed. Session lasts up to 30 minutes.

4

Expert logs outcomes

Post-session, expert submits anonymized outcome: advice given, referral recommended (yes/no), case severity rating. This feeds the data dashboard and triggers any necessary escalation workflow.

5

Follow-up SMS at 7 days

Survivor receives automated check-in: "Did you get the help you needed?" One-tap response. Non-response flags case for CHW outreach. Survivor can re-book another expert session at any time.

6

Expert vetting and quality assurance

All experts are verified through partner NGOs (Rainbo, AdvocAid), hold relevant professional qualifications, complete HERLINE 232 safeguarding training, and sign a data confidentiality agreement. Expert performance tracked via survivor feedback scores (anonymous).

Safeguarding note - critical for evaluation committee: The anonymous bridged call model means HERLINE 232 holds the technical infrastructure and the expert network, while survivors retain full control of what they disclose. No session can be escalated without explicit survivor consent. All experts operate under the NGO lead's safeguarding policy. This is not a hotline - it is a structured, accountable, consent-based consultation service.
Complete Feature Set

Every capability, in full

01

Multi-channel intake core

SMS short code, USSD *232# menu, WhatsApp Business bot, Voice IVR, and web portal - five simultaneous channels. Works on basic phones with no internet. 24/7 availability. Immediate automated acknowledgement on all channels within 60 seconds of contact.

02

Intelligent 3-question triage core

Every incoming contact is assessed for urgency via three safety questions. Outputs one of three levels: Emergency (immediate physical danger - triggers instant responder alert), Active Support Needed (queued for CHW within 4 hours), Information Only (automated legal/rights content delivered). High-risk cases involving children or imminent FGM ceremony trigger a priority override.

03

SafeVoice expert consultation network new

Full anonymous expert scheduling and bridged call system as described above. Six expert types. Schedulable via SMS, USSD, or WhatsApp. Anonymous bridge call architecture - no phone numbers exposed. Post-session outcome logging. Survivor feedback scoring for expert quality assurance.

04

Community health worker response team core

40 trained CHWs receive assigned cases via lightweight Android app. Log field visits, update case status, escalate to coordinators. Works on 2G networks. Offline mode syncs when connectivity restored. CHWs are the human bridge between digital platform and physical community - the layer that reaches women who never self-report.

05

CEFM and FGM/C early alert module new

Community members - teachers, neighbours, CHWs, family members - can anonymously flag a girl at immediate risk of forced marriage or FGM ceremony without reporting their own identity. Alert goes to NGO coordinator within 15 minutes. Triggers rapid response protocol with district-specific contact list. Designed specifically for Koinadugu and Kambia districts where rates are highest and communities are most aware of upcoming ceremonies.

06

Structured follow-up protocol new

Not a one-time interaction. Every case receives automated SMS check-ins at day 7 and day 30. Non-response to day-7 check-in automatically flags case for CHW field outreach. Survivor can re-engage, re-book an expert session, or update their status at any time using their case token. Platform retains case responsibility until resolution is confirmed - no survivor is abandoned mid-journey.

07

Real-time NGO case management dashboard core

Web-based portal for NGO coordinators and field supervisors. View all active cases by district, urgency, type, and status. Assign CHWs to cases. Track referral outcomes. Generate donor reports. Role-based access: field officers see district only, coordinators see full picture, data officers see anonymized analytics. Offline-capable for low-connectivity environments.

08

Anonymized incident analytics engine data

All cases aggregate into real-time visual dashboards: breakdown by type (sexual assault, domestic violence, CEFM, FGM/C), age group, district, time of day, channel used, referral outcome, expert session uptake, and follow-up response rate. Data is disaggregated by gender, age, and disability status. Fully anonymized - no individual can be identified from analytics output.

09

GBVIMS+ data integration data

HERLINE 232 data exports in a format compatible with Sierra Leone's national Gender-Based Violence Information Management System (GBVIMS+, launched 2023). This makes HERLINE 232 not just a local project tool but a contributor to the national SGBV evidence infrastructure - a major differentiator for the evaluation committee, demonstrating systemic impact beyond the grant period.

10

Advocacy report generator data

One-click generation of structured district-level SGBV reports formatted for submission to the Ministry of Gender and Children's Affairs, local councils, and CFLI donor reporting requirements. Four reports generated annually. Translates platform data directly into policy-grade evidence. This closes the loop: survivors report → data collected → evidence generated → advocacy submitted → policy changes → fewer survivors.

11

Legal information library core

Built-in, plain-language repository of survivor rights under Sierra Leone law: Sexual Offences Act 2012 (amended 2019), Domestic Violence Act 2007, Child Rights Act 2007, and Prohibition of Child Marriage Act 2024. Delivered via any channel without needing to report an incident. Summaries written in Krio by community co-designers. Audio versions available via voice IVR for non-literate users.

12

Quick-exit / panic feature new

On WhatsApp bot and web portal, a single tap instantly clears the screen and replaces it with neutral content (a weather page). Designed for survivors in homes where an abuser may see their phone. Session is paused - not lost - and can be resumed later with the case token. On USSD, pressing "0" at any point exits immediately with no trace in call logs.

13

Multilingual interface core

Full Krio and English on all channels. Voice IVR includes Temne (Northern Province) and Mende (Eastern Province). WhatsApp bot accepts voice note messages for non-literate users - transcribed by CHW before processing. All messaging co-designed with community members, not translated by outsiders. Legal content reviewed by local paralegals for accuracy and cultural appropriateness.

14

Service directory with live capacity status new

Real-time database of verified service providers in each target district: name, location, opening hours, services offered, and - critically - current capacity status (open / full / closed). Updated by NGO staff via the web portal. Survivors are never referred to a closed facility or a service that is at capacity. Eliminates the "broken referral" problem that causes survivors to give up on seeking help.

15

Survivor wellbeing content library new

Beyond crisis reporting - a library of short, audio-friendly content on mental health recovery, economic rights, menstrual health, and safe relationship norms. Delivered proactively to survivors post-case as part of the follow-up protocol. Designed with feminist NGO partners to ensure messaging is empowering, not paternalistic. Available on-demand via WhatsApp or USSD menu.

16

Community sensitization tracker data

Logs all community awareness events - date, location, attendees, gender breakdown, key messages delivered. Links event attendance data to subsequent platform uptake in the same community, allowing the team to measure which outreach approaches drive the most engagement. Feeds the KAP (Knowledge, Attitude, Practice) survey pre/post comparison required for the SMART indicators.

Escalation Architecture

What we handle. What we pass on.

HERLINE 232 has clear boundaries - and that is a strength, not a weakness. We own the intake, triage, expert consultation, follow-up, and evidence generation. When a case requires physical presence, medical intervention, legal prosecution, or long-term shelter, we escalate with full documented context to the appropriate implementing body. No cold handoffs. No lost cases.

Emergency Police Family Support Unit (FRU) - Active assault, immediate physical danger, life threat. HERLINE 232 alerts FRU directly and remains on follow-up. 0-2 hrs · HERLINE alerts
Medical Rainbo Initiative / One-Stop Centres - Forensic examination, emergency contraception, STI treatment, evidence preservation. Case file transferred with survivor consent. 0-24 hrs · Warm transfer
Legal AdvocAid / Legal Aid Board - Formal case documentation, court support, paralegal assistance. Expert session may precede this referral to prepare survivor for legal process. 48-72 hrs · With case file
Policy Ministry of Gender and Children's Affairs (MoGCA) - Aggregated data and advocacy reports submitted quarterly. Systemic patterns flagged for policy response. GBVIMS+ data feed maintained. Quarterly · Data reports
Long-term Women's Empowerment Partners / Safe Shelters - Survivors fleeing domestic situations needing housing, livelihood support, or long-term psychosocial rehabilitation beyond what HERLINE 232 can sustain. Ongoing · Tracked
What makes our escalation different: Every referral carries a structured case summary - type of incident, expert session outcomes, triage level, language preference, and district. The receiving body never starts from zero. And HERLINE 232 sends a follow-up to the survivor at 30 days regardless of where their case has gone, to confirm the referral was fulfilled. We are accountable for the handoff, not just the intake.

Privacy & Safety Architecture

Built for the most vulnerable

Data privacy

  • No names or identifiers ever stored at any point
  • Phone numbers hashed - never stored in readable form
  • Expert calls use anonymous bridge - no number exposure
  • Case tokens are the only link between contacts
  • End-to-end encrypted data transfer on all channels
  • Raw contact data auto-deleted after 90 days
  • Data stored on servers within West Africa region
  • GDPR-aligned architecture as baseline standard

Survivor safety features

  • Quick-exit: one tap clears screen, shows neutral content
  • USSD "0" exits instantly with no call log trace
  • No escalation without explicit survivor consent
  • Survivor controls what expert is told during session
  • Session can be paused and resumed - never forced
  • CHW outreach is opt-in - not automatic for all cases
  • All messaging avoids language that could endanger
  • Expert safeguarding policy - signed by all network members
