HERLINE 232

Safe Voices, Fast Action - A multi-channel SGBV incident reporting and survivor referral platform for Sierra Leone.

SMS · USSD · WhatsApp · Voice No smartphone required Krio + English MaxCIT Technology Partner 2 districts · 24 months
Overview

What HERLINE 232 is

HERLINE 232 is a survivor-centered digital reporting and referral system that allows women and girls in Sierra Leone to safely report sexual and gender-based violence, access emergency referrals, and receive follow-up support - all from a basic mobile phone, without internet, without a smartphone, and without revealing their identity.

Named after Sierra Leone's international dialing code (+232), the platform operates across five channels simultaneously, meeting survivors wherever they are - in a rural village, at a market, in a home. It connects each report directly to the nearest verified service provider: a Rainbo Centre, a legal aid paralegal, a One-Stop Centre, or a trained community health worker.

On the back end, every anonymized interaction feeds a real-time data dashboard that gives NGO staff, policymakers, and advocates the evidence they need to drive systemic change.

Access Channels

Five ways to reach HERLINE 232

💬

SMS

Text a keyword to a short code. Works on any basic phone. No data needed. Receives automated triage response within 60 seconds.

📟

USSD Menu

Dial *232# to access a guided menu - like mobile banking. Works offline. No literacy required for navigation. Available 24/7.

📱

WhatsApp Bot

For urban and peri-urban users with data access. Conversational interface in Krio and English. Supports images and voice notes.

📞

Voice IVR

Call the helpline and navigate an audio menu. Ideal for low-literacy survivors. Supports Krio, English, and key local dialects.

🌐

Web Portal

For NGO case workers and community health workers to log cases, update referrals, and access the data dashboard.

User Journey

What happens when someone reaches out

STEP 01

Contact

Survivor texts, dials *232#, or calls. No name required. Immediate acknowledgement sent.

STEP 02

Triage

Automated system asks 3 safety questions. Assesses urgency level: emergency / support needed / information only.

STEP 03

Route

System identifies nearest verified service by district - Rainbo Centre, legal aid, One-Stop Centre, CHW.

STEP 04

Connect

Survivor receives contact details or direct warm handoff to responder within the 48-hour target window.

STEP 05

Follow-up

Automated check-in SMS at 7 days. Case status updated in dashboard. Survivor can re-engage at any point.

Referral Pathway

Where cases are directed

Emergency Police FRU (Family Support Unit) - Immediate physical danger, active assault, life threat 0-2 hrs
Medical Rainbo Initiative Centre / One-Stop Centre - Medical examination, forensic evidence, emergency contraception, STI treatment 0-24 hrs
Psychosocial Trained Community Health Workers / NGO Counsellors - Trauma support, safe house referral, psychosocial first aid 24-48 hrs
Legal AdvocAid / Legal Aid Board - Case documentation, court support, paralegal assistance under Sexual Offences Act 2012 48-72 hrs
Economic Women's Empowerment Partners - Livelihood support, safe housing referral for survivors fleeing domestic situations Ongoing
Platform Features

Every feature, in detail

01

Anonymous reporting engine

No name, no ID, no location data stored. Each case is assigned a randomized case token the survivor can use to check status or follow up. Phone numbers are hashed and never stored in plaintext. GDPR-aligned data architecture.

02

Intelligent triage system

Three-question safety assessment determines urgency level automatically. High-risk cases (immediate danger, child involved, FGM ceremony imminent) trigger priority alert to the nearest responder. Low-risk cases receive information and are queued for CHW follow-up.

03

District-based service directory

Real-time database of verified service providers across target districts - name, address, opening hours, services offered, and capacity status. Updated by NGO staff via web portal. Survivors are always matched to the nearest available provider, never a closed or full facility.

04

Automated follow-up SMS

System sends a check-in message 7 days after first contact. Simple one-tap response: "I got help" / "I still need help" / "I am safe." Non-responses flag the case for CHW outreach. Survivors can opt out of follow-up at any time.

05

NGO case management dashboard

Web-based portal for trained NGO staff and CHWs. View incoming cases, update referral status, flag high-risk cases, and generate reports. Role-based access: field officers see their district only; coordinators see full picture. Offline-capable for areas with poor connectivity.

06

Real-time incident analytics

Anonymized data aggregated into visual dashboards: cases by type, age group, district, time of day, referral outcome, and follow-up response rate. Data disaggregated by gender, disability status, and age. Exportable for donor reporting and national SGBV data integration (GBVIMS+).

07

Multilingual interface

Full support for Krio (primary) and English across all channels. Voice IVR includes Temne and Mende language options for rural districts. WhatsApp bot supports voice note submissions for non-literate users. Translation managed in collaboration with local community members.

08

Community health worker app

Lightweight mobile app (Android, works on low-end devices) for trained CHWs to receive assigned cases, log field visits, update case notes, and escalate to coordinators. Works on 2G. Syncs when connectivity is restored after offline use.

09

Panic/quick-exit feature

On the WhatsApp bot and web version, a single tap exits and clears the screen immediately - replacing it with a weather page or neutral content. Designed for survivors in homes where an abuser may see their phone. Session is paused, not lost.

10

Legal information library

Built-in repository of survivor rights under Sierra Leone law - Sexual Offences Act 2012, Domestic Violence Act 2007, Prohibition of Child Marriage Act 2024. Plain-language summaries in Krio. Accessible via all channels without needing to report an incident.

11

CEFM and FGM/C early alert module

Community members (teachers, neighbours, CHWs) can anonymously flag a girl at risk of forced marriage or FGM ceremony. Alert goes to NGO coordinator and triggers rapid response protocol. Specifically designed for the Koinadugu and Kambia districts where prevalence is highest.

12

Data export and advocacy tools

One-click generation of district-level SGBV reports formatted for submission to the Ministry of Gender and Children's Affairs, local councils, and GBVIMS+ national database. Designed to turn platform data directly into policy advocacy evidence - four reports per year built into the project plan.

Privacy & Language

Safety and accessibility by design

Privacy architecture

  • No names or identifiers ever stored
  • Phone numbers hashed - not readable
  • Case tokens used for follow-up only
  • End-to-end encrypted data transfer
  • Quick-exit / screen-clear on web/WhatsApp
  • Data stored on servers within West Africa
  • Role-based access - no single person sees all
  • Auto-delete of raw data after 90 days

Language support

  • Krio - primary language, all channels
  • English - all channels
  • Temne - voice IVR (Northern districts)
  • Mende - voice IVR (Eastern districts)
  • Voice note support for non-literate users
  • Audio-guided USSD menus
  • Plain-language legal summaries in Krio
  • Community co-design of all messaging
